Adidas shares rise after 'supershoe' helps seal sub-two hour marathon win

STORY: Adidas shares rose on Monday (April 27) the day after a historic racing win. Investors bought in after Kenya's Sabastian Sawe became the first person to run a marathon in under two hours in an official race. He won the London Marathon wearing Adidas' Adizero Adios Pro Evo 3 trainers. Sawe finished the race in one hour 59 minutes and 30 seconds. The sub‑two‑hour marathon has been one of sport's biggest goals for years. Ethiopia's Yomif Kejelcha finished second in his marathon debut on Sunday. While Tigst Assefa broke her own women-only world record. All three were wearing the same make of Adidas trainers, which are due to go on sale on Thursday for a reported $500. The win is a boost for Adidas against arch-rival Nike. It comes after years of lab‑backed experiments and near‑misses looking to build a so-called "supershoe" to break the record. Nike's Breaking2 attempt at Monza in 2017 just fell short. Although Kenyan great Eliud Kipchoge ran under two hours in INEOS's 1:59 Challenge two years later. But those efforts fell outside the sport's official record books. Adidas said the Pro Evo 3 weighs an average 30% less than its predecessor and improves running economy by 1.6%. Adidas shares were up 2% in mid-morning trade, although they're down close to a fifth since the start of this year. That's due to concerns about the group's exposure to U.S. tariffs and the impact of the Middle East conflict.
