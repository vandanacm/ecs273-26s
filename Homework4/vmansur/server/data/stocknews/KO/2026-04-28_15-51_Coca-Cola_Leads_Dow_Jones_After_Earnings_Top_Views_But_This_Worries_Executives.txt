Coca-Cola shares gapped above a buy point after the soda giant beat first-quarter expectations. The stock is the top performer Tuesday in the Dow Jones Industrial Average. The world's largest soft drink company reported a 12% increase in sales to $12.5 billion, accelerating from a range of 1% to 7% in the previous four quarters.
